What I Know About Creativity

From reading and researching so much about creativity, I know that the one definitive
thing about creativity is that it is utilized in all fields and exists everywhere.
While traditionally, creativity is usually seen in products like paintings, poems, and music,
it spans farther than this. As creativity is the use of imagination, background knowledge,
divergent thinking, problem-solving, and emotional expression to think of or create novel ideas
and products, it must be apparent in every field or the field would not evolve. For example,
creativity is needed just as much in construction to continuously find better ways to build things,
as art to create new compositions.

  In Creativity Creep, Joshua Rothman exemplifies this by quoting a list of creative products:
“books, dances, radio shows, art exhibitions, software programs, advertising campaigns, hardware innovations,
music compositions, public policies (written or implemented), leadership positions, invited lectures, and buildings
designed” (Rothman, 2014). Although products like advertising campaigns and public policies are not conventionally creative,
without creativity in these domains we would see the same marketing strategies and policies that we did in the past century,
yet they are ever-evolving. The span of domains that creativity is necessary for is endless and proven time and time again in history,
from the formulation of the structure of DNA to Picasso’s creation of Guernica, and will continue to be proven as all domains evolve
(Weisburg 2010).
